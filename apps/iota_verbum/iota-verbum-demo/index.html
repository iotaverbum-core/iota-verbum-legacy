How this demo works

  1. Ask the model anything. The chat behaves like a normal large language model (simulated here in your browser).
  2. Then click “Run through Iota gate”. We send your question and the model’s answer into a small Iota Verbum demo schema.
  3. See the attestation. Iota checks the answer against four boundaries – love, justice, holiness, beauty – and returns an attestation: allowed or held at boundary, plus a short mirror of what you can learn about God and your own heart.

This is a demo. In production, the gate would be enforced in the backend – no email sent, no loan approved, no alert raised – unless the attestation passes.

A coherent conscience for an incoherent AI world.

Most AI governance is a moving target. Iota Verbum anchors behaviour in something that doesn’t move – the character of the triune God.

Iota Verbum is not trying to replace your models or your people. It stands between the two as a covenant-style gate:

  • It asks who you are claiming to be (□ identity).
  • It asks what you are actually doing (◇ enactment).
  • It asks what this will do to people, creation, and trust (Δ effect).

When a decision violates love, justice, holiness, or beauty, the gate stays shut and says why. When a decision passes, it does so with a signed attestation, not a shrug.
