How it works
Policy → Code → Proof → Formation in one grammar
iota Verbum turns your values and constraints into an executable modal charter. Each decision is checked at runtime, attested, and logged with a formation snapshot.

1 · Charter – Express who you say you are

Work with risk, product, compliance and ethics teams to translate:

  • values and mission statements,
  • hard constraints (regulation, credit policy),
  • and hardship / vulnerability rules

into a modal charter using:

□ must – identity & hard rules
◇ may – permitted actions
Δ effect – outcomes you own
Λ lament – how you hold unresolved harm
The charter becomes the spec that every BNPL / alt-credit decision is checked against before it goes live.

2 · Execution & attestation – Runtime theorem prover

Your existing credit models call the iota Verbum API with a DecisionContext. The engine:

  • evaluates the charter against that context,
  • runs hardship checks (Axiom Λ) and participation assessments (Λ/Β/Ρ/Μ/Σ),
  • produces a verdict plus a proof-carrying attestation payload.
Traditional AI governance monitors metrics after the fact. iota Verbum runs as a runtime theorem prover for each decision and emits a record that boards, auditors, and regulators can read.
Why formation-first
Optimization asks “Did it work?” Formation asks “Who did this make us?”
Most AI tooling optimizes for profit, accuracy, or risk-adjusted return. iota Verbum adds a second-order question: over hundreds of decisions, what kind of institution are you becoming?

The participation layer tracks five axes for each decision stream:

  • Lament (Λ) – do you recognise and attend to genuine hardship?
  • Blessing (Β) – do you treat surplus as gift to steward, or entitlement to extract?
  • Repentance (Ρ) – do you name and repair harm, or just manage PR?
  • Mission (Μ) – are you serving real people and communities, or only metrics?
  • Sanctification / Integrity (Σ) – do you remain principled under pressure?

Each attestation carries a participation snapshot, compressing these axes to simple 0–3 scores for dashboards and reports, with deeper notes available for internal review.

Over time, leadership can see not just whether a portfolio is performing, but whether it is forming the institution toward or away from the identity it claims.
Origins
Proven first on the hardest test case: Scripture
Under the hood, iota Verbum’s logic began as computational theology – a way of reading the Gospel of Mark as executable moral reasoning – before being applied to credit, healthcare, and law.

The core modal grammar:

□L → ◇E L(t) → Δ → ⊗Λ
Necessary love (□L), enacted in time (◇E), bearing real effects (Δ), holding the gap with authorised lament (⊗Λ).

This grammar was first stress-tested on the Gospel of Mark, encoding each pericope as a λ-document with identity invariants, enacted episodes, and observed effects.

That same structure turns out to be exactly what institutions need if they want to remain coherent with their stated values under economic and political pressure.

You do not need to adopt the theology to benefit from the engine, but you gain an alignment framework that has been argued over in a canonical context for centuries, not just tuned on internet data.
