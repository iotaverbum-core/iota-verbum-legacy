If someone asks a hard question online…

…Reach doesn’t send them to a random chatbot. Iota Verbum answers under a “Reach Rule” – a small, church-owned sovereignty layer that ties answers back to Scripture, doctrine and pastoral wisdom.

Attested AI for Reach (vision)

Sovereign AI · Under Reach Rule

Member’s anonymous question:

“I feel like I only ever ‘half see’ what Jesus is doing. I’m scared that I’ll never properly change. What if I stay stuck like this forever?”

Reach Answer (example):

You’re not alone in feeling that way. In Mark 8:22–26, the man’s healing comes in two stages. Jesus does that on purpose, not because he’s weak, but to show his disciples (and us) that spiritual sight often clears slowly. The important thing is not how fast you “see”, but that you stay with Jesus and don’t go back to the old village.

It may help to: • keep bringing your confusion to him in prayer, • stay close to a community that will remind you of his grace, and • remember that the One who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion (Philippians 1:6).

This answer is not a diagnosis or replacement for pastoral care. If this lands deeply, please speak to an elder or trusted Christian friend.

Scriptures used
Mark 8:22–26; Philippians 1:6; pattern of gradual sight in the Gospels.
Doctrinal anchors
Trinity · Grace · Sanctification · Perseverance
Policy checks (summary)
ReachRule.gospel-centered = true
ReachRule.no-medical-advice = passed
ReachRule.escalate-if-self-harm = not triggered
Accountability
Answer traceable to: sermon index (“Mark 8:22–26 – ‘Don’t go back to the village’”), Reach doctrinal statement, and this member’s privacy settings.
